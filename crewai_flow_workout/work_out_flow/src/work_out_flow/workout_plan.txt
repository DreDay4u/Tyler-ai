**General Workout Plan**

**Objective:** This workout plan is designed to improve overall fitness, including strength, endurance, and flexibility. It is suitable for beginners to intermediate individuals. Adjust the intensity and weights according to your fitness level.

**Weekly Schedule:**

- **Monday: Full Body Strength Training**
  - Warm-up: 5-10 minutes of light cardio (jogging, cycling)
  - Squats: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
  - Push-ups: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
  - Bent-over Rows: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
  - Plank: 3 sets of 30-60 seconds
  - Cool down: Stretching exercises for major muscle groups

- **Tuesday: Cardio and Core**
  - Warm-up: 5-10 minutes of dynamic stretching
  - Running or brisk walking: 20-30 minutes
  - Bicycle Crunches: 3 sets of 15 reps
  - Russian Twists: 3 sets of 15 reps
  - Leg Raises: 3 sets of 10 reps
  - Cool down: Yoga or Pilates session (15 minutes)

- **Wednesday: Rest or Active Recovery**
  - Light activities such as walking, yoga, or swimming

- **Thursday: Upper Body Strength Training**
  - Warm-up: 5-10 minutes of light cardio
  - Dumbbell Bench Press: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
  - Pull-ups or Assisted Pull-ups: 3 sets of 8-10 reps
  - Shoulder Press: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
  - Tricep Dips: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
  - Cool down: Stretching exercises for upper body

- **Friday: Lower Body and Core**
  - Warm-up: 5-10 minutes of light cardio
  - Lunges: 3 sets of 10 reps per leg
  - Deadlifts: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
  - Calf Raises: 3 sets of 15 reps
  - Plank with Leg Lift: 3 sets of 10 reps per leg
  - Cool down: Stretching exercises for lower body

- **Saturday: Flexibility and Balance**
  - Yoga or Pilates class/session: 30-60 minutes
  - Focus on deep stretching and balance exercises

- **Sunday: Rest or Light Activity**
  - Engage in a leisure activity like walking, cycling, or swimming

**Notes:**
- Ensure proper form and technique to prevent injuries.
- Stay hydrated and maintain a balanced diet to support your fitness goals.
- Adjust the number of sets, reps, and rest periods based on your progress and fitness level.
- Consult with a fitness professional if you have any health concerns or specific goals.
