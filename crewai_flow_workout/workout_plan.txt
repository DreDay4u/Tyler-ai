**General Workout Plan**

**Objective:** This workout plan is designed to improve overall fitness, including strength, endurance, and flexibility. It is suitable for beginners with no specific information provided.

**Duration:** 4 weeks  
**Frequency:** 4 days per week

**Day 1: Full Body Strength Training**  
- Warm-up: 5-10 minutes of light cardio (jogging, jumping jacks)  
- Squats: 3 sets of 10-12 reps  
- Push-ups: 3 sets of 8-10 reps  
- Bent-over Rows (using dumbbells or resistance bands): 3 sets of 10-12 reps  
- Plank: 3 sets of 30-60 seconds hold  
- Cool down: 5-10 minutes of stretching

**Day 2: Cardio and Core**  
- Warm-up: 5-10 minutes of brisk walking or cycling  
- Interval Training: 20 minutes (1 minute sprint, 2 minutes walk/jog)  
- Bicycle Crunches: 3 sets of 15 reps  
- Russian Twists: 3 sets of 15 reps per side  
- Leg Raises: 3 sets of 10 reps  
- Cool down: 5-10 minutes of stretching

**Day 3: Rest or Active Recovery**  
- Light activities such as walking, yoga, or swimming

**Day 4: Upper Body and Core**  
- Warm-up: 5-10 minutes of light cardio  
- Dumbbell Shoulder Press: 3 sets of 10-12 reps  
- Tricep Dips: 3 sets of 10-12 reps  
- Bicep Curls: 3 sets of 10-12 reps  
- Side Plank: 3 sets of 30 seconds per side  
- Cool down: 5-10 minutes of stretching

**Day 5: Lower Body and Cardio**  
- Warm-up: 5-10 minutes of light cardio  
- Lunges: 3 sets of 10 reps per leg  
- Deadlifts (using dumbbells): 3 sets of 10-12 reps  
- Calf Raises: 3 sets of 15 reps  
- Jump Rope: 5 minutes  
- Cool down: 5-10 minutes of stretching

**Day 6 & 7: Rest or Active Recovery**  
- Engage in light activities such as walking, yoga, or swimming

**Notes:**  
- Ensure proper form to prevent injury.  
- Adjust weights and intensity according to your fitness level.  
- Stay hydrated and maintain a balanced diet to support your fitness goals.  
- Consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new exercise program, especially if you have any pre-existing health conditions.
